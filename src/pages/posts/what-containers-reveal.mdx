Seven stories told through everyday document formats. Each piece uses a single container — a form designed for utility, not expression — and asks what it reveals about a life when read in full.

The search bar is more honest than a diary. The voicemail can't be unsent. The prescription can't lie. The grocery list isn't trying to communicate at all. The saved listing is a life tried on in your head. The running log betrays what silence protects. The medical chart holds two voices — the patient and the doctor — and the story lives in the gap between them.

2. **[Voicemails](/posts/voicemails)** — Thirteen saved messages on a phone retrieved during estate settlement.

3. **[Prescription History](/posts/prescription-history)** — Seventeen years of pharmacy records for one family.

4. **[Grocery Lists](/posts/grocery-lists)** — Five years of shopping lists found in a kitchen junk drawer.

5. **[Saved Listings](/posts/saved-listings)** — Fourteen apartments saved to Favorites over eighteen months.

6. **[Strava Routes](/posts/strava-routes)** — Four years and 1,024 laps of the same 5.1km loop.

7. **[Medical Charts](/posts/medical-charts)** — Twenty-six years of one patient file, two doctors.
